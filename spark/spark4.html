Night Life

The Old Thatch, Killeagh

This Cork pub is believed to be the oldest thatched pub in Ireland - and has been in the same family since way back in the 1600s.
But it's not back in the dark ages and its pub grub and friendly atmosphere make this historical spot a must-visit.
The Auld Dubliner, Temple Bar

This Dublin boozer is popular with tourists and locals alike and is renowned for its Guiness - with live music and a great atmosphere to boot.
Charlie’s Bar

You can hardly argue with the fact that this Mayo bar is owned by one of The Chieftains - and if he's not on tour, Matt will
be there for the sessions. Have you left yet?
Dennehy’s Bar
Mary's Bar and Hardware, Wicklow Street

Confused by the hardware reference? Mary's is a cosy spot on Dublin's Wicklow Street - that just happens to be both a bar and
a fully functioning hardware shop which has got to be seen to be believed
The Beach Bar, Sligo

Does exactly what it says on the tin - the thatched bar sits right on the Atlantic Ocean at the unspoiled Aughris Head and goes back
a whopping 300 years.
Gus O'Connor's, Doolin
John Benny's, Dingle

If you're heading to the Kingdom, this has got to be on your list. If you fancy a bed for the night, they even have their own self-catering apartments.
Downes, Waterford

In the same family for six generations, Downes still bottles its own whiskey, and even has a squash court on the premises. Definitely one for the list if you're in the sunny South East.
Callanan's, Cork

This is a true gem nestled on Sullivan's Quay in Cork - and it's like a rural bar in the middle of the city. On the nights cards are being played,
prepare to be told unceremoniously to move if you are sitting in a player's seat and there might even be a game of rings going. And it has got
to be one of the last pubs in Ireland with the beer kegs still under the bar counter.
